engaging with the experience

What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience?

Image

The first thing I paid attention to was the interactive aspect of the site that goes beyond the limits of the browser screen, with the first prompt asking “resize your browser to make the sun rise”. Changing the content along with the resizing of the browser was a technique I had never encountered before and I found that very intriguing.

Spend two minutes with the experience and create a list of each of your discrete actions.

Image
  • follow tutorial prompt and drag the wheel to progress
  • click around for anything interactive
  • drag around the scene to see more
  • enjoy the background music
  • read the poems available
  • held down click and immersed myself in the sound design

What part of the experience did you spend the most time engaging with?

Image

I spent most time engaging with the “find the secret feature” aspect of this experience. I really like interactive aspects of games and sites where I can click on objects and receive a response in return, it really helps me feel immersed in the environment by allowing interactions between me and the scenery.

What was the most common action in your two minute interaction with the experience?

Image

I was mostly dragging around the screen, after the tutorial explained that there was “secret features” in the environment, it prompted me to try and look around the entire scene to find that interactive element. Other than that, I read the poems and moved along each page at around the same pace.

Primary goal of the experience

What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experience?

Image

To me, it seemed like the creator wanted the audience to experience “the essence of a summer day” through their perspective. In the introduction page, it is also stated that this site is “a tribute to the grand unveiling of the RM 07-01 coloured ceramics”, which after a quick search; is revealed to be a summer collection of bold-coloured wrist watches.

What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time? (For how long and how many different times)

Image

I feel that this is an experience that can last for quite a while, I was able to spent around 10 minutes just to get through the very first section (finishing the Archipelago). However I’m unsure about the replayability of this site as it is quite a simple and straightforward experience. Furthermore, I find that there wasn’t anymore “secret features” in the later pages which made it less engaging.

How does the interactive experience communicate how it should be interacted with over time?

Image

It mainly communicates its interactive elements through direct written instructions, implemented into a skippable tutorial. Due to the simple nature of the interactions, not much more is communicated after the tutorial is finished, it’s expected that the user understands how to interact with the rest of the experience once the tutorial is over.

Other media forms referenced

What other media forms (digital or otherwise) does the experience reference?

Image

personally, this experience reminds me a lot of point and click games where you interact with elements on screen to progress the story. Although there was not a lot of interactivity in terms of the elements in the illustrations, the act of having the player experience the story at their own pace reminded me of the role playing games and visual novel genres.

What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should act when engaging with your research experience?

Image

The site referencing point and click games indicates to the user that they should drag around to explore and interact with the environment, this also makes the experience feel a lot more natural to people who has previous experience playing these sorts of games.

What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should feel when engaging with your research experience?

Image

The reference to point and click games makes the user feel that they should explore and interact with everything in the environment, ensuring that the user is able to explore the environment fully and properly get immersed in the summer atmosphere.

Frustration and satisfaction

What is the most frustrating part of the interaction to you and what makes that part frustrating?

Image

It was actually slightly frustrating to be forced to drag around the environment rather than having the full environment on a full computer screen. Although I found the resizing browser interaction to be very creative and fun, it was quite irritating to have to drag around the screen to view the full environment.

What is the most satisfying part of the interaction to you and what makes that part satisfying?

Image

The most satisfying part of the interaction was finding the “secret features” (clicking on an object with a dim glow around the edges). The act of interacting with a part of the environment and receiving the “reward” of a short poem gives me a boost of dopamine from that feeling of discovery.
